You are participating in an experiment on decision-making. You will be paid in cash for your participation, and the exact amount you receive will be determined during the experiment and will depend on your decisions and the decisions of others. You will be paid your earnings privately so that no other participant will find out how much you earn. These earnings will be paid to you at the end of the experiment along with the 5 USD participation payment.

Please read the following instruction carefully. You can check this instruction throughout the experiment. If you have any questions, please raise your hand and the experimenter will answer you privately. Please do not ask any questions aloud.

This experiment consists of 50 rounds, and each round is a separate decision task. We will randomly select one round to count for payment from the entire experiment. Each round is equally likely to be selected. The points you receive from that round will be used to calculate your payment for the experiment, and points will be converted to cash at the rate of 1 USD for every 10 points. More specifically, we will take the total number of points you earned in the round that counts, divide by 10, and then round this amount to the nearest quarter. We will pay you this amount in addition to the 5 USD participation payment and a payment determined by your decision in a lottery choice task.

In this experiment, each participant will be assigned to one of two roles: S or R. Please refer to the next part of the instruction for your assigned role. Your role will remain fixed throughout the experiment, and you will be provided with information specific to your role.

Before the experiment, you will be randomly matched with one other participant. In every pair of participants, there will be one player in each role (one S player and one R player). The pair will remain the same throughout the experiment.

Note that you will not know the identity of the other participant you are matched with, and your earnings for each round depend only on your decision in that round and the decision of the participant you are matched with in that round.

In this experiment, there are two buckets, Gold and Silver, each containing 20 balls of the color of Gold and Silver. In the silver bucket, there are 12 silver balls and 8 golden balls. In the golden bucket, there are 6 silver balls and 14 golden balls.

In each round of the experiment, a bucket is randomly chosen.

From the chosen bucket, Player S will draw a ball. If the ball is Gold, S does not need to do any further action. If the ball is Silver, S should choose whether to report the color to Player R.

After observing Player S’s action, either reporting Silver or remaining silent, Player R needs to guess the color of the bucket, Gold and Silver.

After Player R’s action, this round ends, both players will observe their payoffs of this round, and continue playing for an extra 49 rounds (so overall 50 rounds). After these decision tasks, you will complete a lottery choice task. You need to make 10 decisions, and one of them will be realized. The points you earn will also be converted to cash at the rate of 1 USD for every 10 points and included in your final payment. After completing a post-experiment survey, you will receive the payment. The experiment will take approximately 70 minutes.
